But here's the thing: there's a massive gap between the potential of AI in manufacturing and its actual implementation. Why? Because teams need people who understand both worlds - the physical reality of manufacturing and the digital possibilities of AI.

The PROJECT FORGE Journey

Six months ago, Engineers started PROJECT FORGE - an 18-month self-directed PhD-level curriculum in industrial AI. The goal? Transform from a steel production supervisor into an industrial AI specialist. Not by leaving manufacturing behind, but by bringing these worlds together.

The curriculum covers:

  • Mathematical Foundations: Linear algebra, calculus, statistics, optimization theory
  • Core AI/ML: Deep learning, reinforcement learning, computer vision, NLP
  • Industrial Applications: Process control, quality prediction, prescriptive analytics
  • Domain Expertise: Metallurgy, thermodynamics, operations research
  • Practical Skills: Python, R, SQL, cloud computing, MLOps

What is edikan.ai?

This platform is three things:

  1. A Learning Journey: This guide will be documenting every step of a typical PROJECT FORGE journey, sharing what works, what doesn't, and everything in between.
  2. Interactive Tutorials: Not just articles, but actual coding exercises you can run in your browser. Learn by doing, not just reading.
  3. A Community: A place for manufacturing professionals, data scientists, and anyone interested in industrial AI to learn together.

Why Learn in Public?

There's something powerful about learning in public. It creates accountability, forces clarity of thought, and most importantly, helps others on similar journeys. Every mistake Engineers make, every breakthrough Engineers have, becomes a learning opportunity for the community.

"The best way to learn is to teach. The best way to teach is to learn in public."
