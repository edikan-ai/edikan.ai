Launching edikan.ai: My Journey from Steel Mills to Software

September 2, 2025 • 5 min read • By Edikan Udofa

Today marks a significant milestone in my journey. After months of preparation through PROJECT FORGE, I'm launching edikan.ai - an interactive platform for learning industrial AI. This isn't just another tech blog; it's my commitment to learning in public and helping others transition into the incredible world of industrial AI.

Why Industrial AI?

For the past several years, I've been a Production Supervisor at Nucor Steel, managing complex manufacturing processes and analyzing vast amounts of operational data. I've seen firsthand how AI and machine learning can transform manufacturing - from predictive maintenance that prevents million-dollar breakdowns to optimization algorithms that reduce energy consumption by 15%.

But here's the thing: there's a massive gap between the potential of AI in manufacturing and its actual implementation. Why? Because we need people who understand both worlds - the physical reality of manufacturing and the digital possibilities of AI.

The PROJECT FORGE Journey

Six months ago, I started PROJECT FORGE - an 18-month self-directed PhD-level curriculum in industrial AI. The goal? Transform from a steel production supervisor into an industrial AI specialist. Not by leaving manufacturing behind, but by bringing these worlds together.

The curriculum covers:

  • Mathematical Foundations: Linear algebra, calculus, statistics, optimization theory
  • Core AI/ML: Deep learning, reinforcement learning, computer vision, NLP
  • Industrial Applications: Process control, quality prediction, prescriptive analytics
  • Domain Expertise: Metallurgy, thermodynamics, operations research
  • Practical Skills: Python, R, SQL, cloud computing, MLOps

What is edikan.ai?

This platform is three things:

  1. A Learning Journey: I'll be documenting every step of my PROJECT FORGE journey, sharing what works, what doesn't, and everything in between.
  2. Interactive Tutorials: Not just articles, but actual coding exercises you can run in your browser. Learn by doing, not just reading.
  3. A Community: A place for manufacturing professionals, data scientists, and anyone interested in industrial AI to learn together.

Why Learn in Public?

There's something powerful about learning in public. It creates accountability, forces clarity of thought, and most importantly, helps others on similar journeys. Every mistake I make, every breakthrough I have, becomes a learning opportunity for the community.

"The best way to learn is to teach. The best way to teach is to learn in public."

What's Next?

Over the coming weeks and months, expect:

  • Weekly deep-dives into industrial AI concepts
  • Real-world case studies from steel manufacturing
  • Interactive Python tutorials with industrial datasets
  • Interviews with experts in manufacturing and AI
  • Open-source tools for industrial data analysis

This is just the beginning. The vision for VERUMCO - the company I'm building - is to become the Renaissance Technologies of industrial AI. But every journey starts with a single step, and today, that step is launching this platform.

Join the Journey

Follow along, try the exercises, share your thoughts, and let's build the future of industrial AI together.
